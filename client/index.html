Big Ideas for HHS

Students are becoming less engaged in the community at HHS for a variety of reasons (some out of our control). However, I think that by targeting certain points we can at least set up a system for following years to be fully engaged in the school once more. The following is a list of improvements or changes that I believe could help to get students back into the community.

Increasing Participation

  • Any event has to be appreciated by the average student participant because they fill the gaps in participation. The more average student participants you engage with, the more likely they are to be comfortable in the community and become high energy participants. High energy participants are the people you can count on to show up to every event and sports game. Even if they aren't fully engaged in the idea, they will be fully engaged with the grade and school community and will make an attempt to enjoy anything put together by the student government.

Spirit Week

  • Spirit week is DYING! Spirit week is incredibly important to the state of the community in the school. It provides a group activity everyone can participate in from teachers and faculty to students in Co2023 to Co2027. A few things make a good pick for a spirit day:

    • Accessible and Inexpensive: Don't choose something that forces excess resources to be used. If they are, it must be minimal.
    • Engagement is a Choice: Everyone has different levels of enthusiasm, and the more hyper specific the day is, the easier it will be to lose the people who would normally have limited spirit.
    • Doesn't interfere with classes more than necessary: HHS is primarily a school and a place of education. However, people who want to go overboard will participate regardless of the day. Strategically placing the more distracting days on half days, or the day of events where distraction exists already is necessary. Controlling the energy will result in less spirit while directing the energy will keep enthusiasm high and let people be creative about spirit days. (On a half day or the last day of spirit week exceptions should be made).
    • Simple Instructions: Spirit days have to be obvious in some way. The average student participant has to be able to put in low effort and still feel like they participated in the day. Good days for this are color wars or red and white day, since anyone can put on the right color to participate. Additionally, anyone who wants to go further easily can do so. Days that limit average student participation are days like dress like a picture of yourself, favorite celebrity day, or adam sandler day. While these are creative it forces a level of participation high enough that you lose all the Average Student Participants.
